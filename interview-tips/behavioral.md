# Behavioral Questions (STAR Method)

## The STAR Method

Structure every behavioral answer as:

| Part        | Description                                              |
|------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| **S**ituation | Set the context — where, when, what was happening     |
| **T**ask      | Describe your specific responsibility or challenge    |
| **A**ction    | Explain what YOU did (not "we")                       |
| **R**esult    | Share the outcome, with metrics if possible           |

---

## Common Behavioral Questions

### Leadership & Ownership

- "Tell me about a time you led a project."
- "Describe a time you took ownership of something outside your role."
- "Tell me about a time you influenced without authority."

### Problem Solving

- "Tell me about a challenging technical problem you solved."
- "Describe a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information."
- "Tell me about a time you had to debug a difficult issue under pressure."

### Collaboration & Conflict

- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a team member or manager."
- "Describe a time you had to work with a difficult colleague."
- "Tell me about a time you gave or received constructive feedback."

### Failure & Learning

- "Tell me about a time you failed. What did you learn?"
- "Describe a project that didn't go as planned."
- "Tell me about a mistake you made and how you handled it."

### Prioritization

- "Tell me about a time you had to manage competing priorities."
- "Describe a situation where you had to say no to a request."
- "Tell me about a time you had to meet a tight deadline."

## Tips

- Prepare 5-8 stories that can be adapted to multiple questions.
- Keep answers to 2-3 minutes.
- Focus on **your** actions and impact, not the team's.
- Use **metrics** when possible ("reduced latency by 30%", "saved 5 hours/week").
- Show what you **learned**, especially in failure stories.
